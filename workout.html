⚠
Your warmup matters more today
Peak estrogen around ovulation may increase ligament laxity slightly. This is not a reason to avoid training -- it is a reason to take every warmup exercise seriously. Complete all warmup movements before loading any weight.
HIIT is more stressful in this phase
Progesterone and cortisol compete for glucocorticoid receptors in mid and late luteal phase, making high intensity exercise significantly more stressful than the calorie burn suggests. The net hormonal cost is higher than the same session in your follicular phase (Hackney 2006).
Consider tempo cardio (20 to 30 minutes at 75 to 80% max HR, zone 3) or moderate steady-state cardio (30 to 40 minutes zone 2) instead. You will get equivalent cardiovascular benefit with significantly less recovery cost.
Building your baseline
Without a confirmed cycle your recommendations reflect a conservative, low-estrogen environment. This is intentional -- it protects your recovery. When your first confirmed cycle begins your recommendations will update automatically to match your actual phase.

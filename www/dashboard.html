Wearable readiness score
Your readiness score may appear lower than expected. Wearable algorithms were calibrated on male physiology. Elevated core temperature and RHR in the luteal phase looks like poor recovery to a device that treats male physiology as the default. Your Em~power recommendations already account for this. (Sims ST. ROAR 2024; De Martin Topranin et al. 2023)
Bone health — your priority right now
Estrogen loss during the menopausal transition accelerates bone density loss. The perimenopausal window is critical — bone can still be built and protected now before estrogen drops further.
What helps most: resistance training with progressive load, impact exercise like brisk walking, calcium 1000mg daily, vitamin D 800 to 1000 IU daily.
Every strength session you log is protecting your bones.
Source: Kohrt WM et al. Medicine and Science in Sports and Exercise 2004.
Metabolism and the menopausal transition
Estrogen loss changes where your body stores fat — from hips and thighs toward the abdomen. This increases insulin resistance. This is not a willpower problem. It is a direct hormonal effect.
What helps: resistance training, protein at every meal, consistent sleep, not skipping meals.
Source: Carr MC. Journal of Clinical Endocrinology and Metabolism 2003. Mauvais-Jarvis et al. Journal of Clinical Investigation 2013.
The hormonal transition and mood
The menopausal transition is associated with the highest rates of depression across the female lifespan. This is a hormonal shift, not a psychological weakness. Estrogen directly regulates serotonin and dopamine.
If you are experiencing significant depression or anxiety please speak to a doctor who understands hormonal mental health. This is treatable.
If you are in crisis right now please contact Crisis Services Canada at 1-833-456-4566 or text 45645.
Source: Freeman EW et al. Archives of General Psychiatry 2004. Bromberger and Epperson. Depression and Anxiety 2018.

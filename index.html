The method

Your body changes every week. Your plan should too.

Most fitness apps treat every day the same. But estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, and cortisol shift dramatically across your cycle — changing your strength, energy, and recovery in ways that are measurable and predictable.

HormoneSync uses at-home saliva testing and smart cycle tracking to build your daily plan around your actual biology. Not a template. Not an average. You.

The science

There's a reason you feel different every week.

01
Some days you're just stronger. Here's why.
Your menstrual cycle has two halves. In the first half, the two weeks after your period ends, a hormone called estrogen rises steadily. Estrogen isn't just a reproductive hormone. It directly boosts your muscle fibres' ability to contract harder and recover faster. Researchers confirmed that measurable muscle strength peaks during this window and drops significantly in the two weeks before your next period. HormoneSync identifies this window and pushes your hardest sessions into it so you get more out of the same effort.
This is your follicular phase
Sarwar, Niclos & Rutherford · Journal of Physiology · 1996
02
Timing your hardest workouts builds more muscle. Same effort, better results.
Not all weeks are equal for building strength. When researchers split women into two groups following the same program, one group doing their hardest sessions in the first half of their cycle and one in the second half, the first group gained significantly more muscle and strength. Their bodies were simply more ready to respond to training. HormoneSync automatically schedules your intensity around this so you are not leaving results on the table.
This is your follicular phase working for you
Kissow et al. · Sports Medicine · 2022
03
There's a short window each month where your body is running at full power. Most women never know it's happening.
About halfway through your cycle, your body releases an egg. Right at this moment, estrogen hits its highest point of the entire month. At the same time, your body produces a brief surge of testosterone. Women produce testosterone too, and in this window it drives power, motivation, and competitiveness in a way that is measurable. Studies show that the connection between your brain and your muscles is at its sharpest right now. Reaction time, coordination, and power output are all elevated. HormoneSync tells you exactly when this window arrives so you can make the most of it.
This is your ovulatory phase
Roth et al. · Journal of Applied Physiology · 2025
04
In the two weeks before your period, the same workout genuinely is harder. That's not in your head.
After ovulation, a hormone called progesterone rises. Progesterone's job is to prepare your body for a potential pregnancy but as a side effect it raises your resting body temperature by up to half a degree. That might sound small, but Yale researchers found it delays your body's ability to sweat and cool itself during exercise. The result is that the exact same run, the same weights, the same class feels measurably harder. Your heart works more. Your perceived effort goes up. Most women think they are having a bad workout. They are actually just in the wrong phase. HormoneSync adjusts your targets so you are training to what your body can actually do that day, not what it could do last week.
This is your luteal phase
Charkoudian & Stachenfeld · Comprehensive Physiology · 2014
05
During your period, your body loses iron. That's why everything feels heavy.
When you bleed, you lose iron, between 10 and 40mg every cycle. Iron is what your red blood cells use to carry oxygen to your muscles. Less iron means less oxygen delivery, which means muscles fatigue faster, effort feels greater, and energy is lower. This compounds if you also exercise regularly, because physical training creates its own iron demand on top of what your period takes. Most women push through this week feeling flat and frustrated, not realising there is a physiological reason. HormoneSync flags this window, reduces your training load automatically, and shifts your nutrition guidance toward iron-replenishing foods so you recover faster and enter your next cycle stronger.
This is your menstrual phase
Angeli et al. · European Journal of Sport Science · 2016
06
In the week before your period, your muscles are harder to protect. Eating enough protein makes the difference.
In the second half of your cycle when progesterone is high, your body becomes less efficient at using amino acids, the building blocks it needs to maintain and repair muscle tissue. This means if you are not eating enough protein during this window, your body starts breaking down muscle to meet its needs instead of building it. You do not need to do anything complicated. You just need to know it is happening and eat accordingly. HormoneSync tracks this phase and adjusts your daily protein targets so your hard-earned muscle stays protected even when your hormones are working against you.
This is your luteal phase asking for more support
Larivière et al. · American Journal of Physiology · 2006

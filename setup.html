You have probably been dismissed before. Maybe your pain was called normal. Maybe your symptoms were called anxiety. Maybe you were told to come back if it got worse. Em~power is not that. Your body sends signals every single day. This app learns to read them and takes every single one seriously.

Your cycle is a vital sign
Your menstrual cycle is a monthly health report. It reflects your bone health, brain health, cardiovascular health, and metabolic health. When it is regular and pain-free your hormonal system is functioning well. When it is not, it is sending you a signal worth listening to.
Built on research about women
Almost everything you have been told about exercise and nutrition was researched on men and generalised to women. Women were not required to be included in research studies until 1993 (NIH Revitalization Act). Em~power is built on research conducted on women.

Perimenopause is one of the most significant hormonal transitions a woman goes through and it is almost entirely ignored by the fitness and health industry. Em~power is built for this.
Source: Freeman EW et al. Archives of General Psychiatry 2004.
When does perimenopause begin?
Perimenopause can begin as early as age 35. The average age of the final menstrual period in Canada is 51. Perimenopause typically begins 4 to 10 years before that.
The single most predictive factor for when you will reach menopause is when your mother did.
Source: Harlow SD et al. Climacteric 2012. STRAW+10 staging.

About hormone replacement therapy
In 2002 a study caused panic about HRT. Headlines said it caused cancer and heart attacks. Millions of women stopped treatment overnight. The study was later found to have been significantly misinterpreted.
Current evidence shows HRT started within 10 years of menopause or before age 60 is associated with reduced risk of osteoporosis, cardiovascular disease, and dementia and significantly improved quality of life.
Em~power is not a substitute for medical advice. If you are experiencing significant symptoms please find a doctor who is current on the evidence.
Source: Manson JE et al. New England Journal of Medicine 2013.
